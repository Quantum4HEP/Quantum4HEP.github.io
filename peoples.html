Marco Lorusso

With a PhD in physics, he has focused primarily on Machine Learning and the implementation of AI on Field Programmable Gate Arrays (FPGAs) for applications in High Energy Physics. His research culminated in a thesis exploring the use of hyper-optimized neural networks deployed on FPGAs to perform anomaly detection, aimed at identifying events beyond the Standard Model, as part of the CMS Collaboration, an experiment at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN. He joined this group in 2023, during his time as a doctoral student at CERN, to assist with technical and programming challenges, and since then, his involvement in the quantum aspects of the work has steadily increased.

Gianluca Bianco

His primary interests include software engineering, quantum computing, high-performance computing, data science, and artificial intelligence. Additionally, he actively contribute to open-source projects, driven by a passion for continuous learning and experimentation with emerging technologies.
